Interesting Astrophysical Places

This is the Andromeda Galaxy, the nearest major galaxy to our own, the Milky Way.
This is the Milky Way, our own galaxy, as seen from Earth! The sky is pretty noice when there is no cities around I guess.
Believed to be the cosmic fire of creation by the Maya of Mesoamerica, this is M42, better known as the Orion Nebula, blazes brightly in the constellation of Orion.
Commonly called the Pleiades or Seven Sisters, M45 is known as an open star cluster. It contains over a thousand stars that are loosely bound by gravity.
"It has been said that astronomy is a humbling and character-building experience. There is perhaps no better demonstration of the folly of human conceits than this distant image of our tiny world. To me, it underscores our responsibility to deal more kindly with one another, and to preserve and cherish the pale blue dot, the only home we've ever known.”
― Carl Sagan, Pale Blue Dot: A Vision of the Human Future in Space
